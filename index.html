CRC is making CU-Boulder a better university
by seeking equitable work conditions for graduate employees.

Graduate employees (including TAs, RAs, and GPTIs) perform a great portion of academic labor that is fundamental to CU-Boulder's statutory mission to be a world-class research and education university. Yet we face many struggles as university employees from insufficient wages to hostile environments.

We envision a fair, inclusive, and democratic campus for all, and pursue actions to continually improve our university.

What We Have Accomplished

Since our formation in late Spring 2016, CRC has…

  • Met with the University Chancellor and the Chief Financial Officer
  • Secured a 6.5% raise for TAs in the 2016-2017 academic year (4.5% above the planned raise)
  • Brought the living wage issue to the attention of the Regents of the CU system
  • Received verbal agreements from the administration to continue to improve compensation, including by improving cost of living calculations for upcoming budget decisions

We’re just getting started! We hope you’ll join us in continuing to improve conditions for CU Boulder graduate students.

Goals For Fall 2016

Equitable pay

CU-Boulder pays its graduate employees approximately 65% of regional self-sufficiency wage, less than any other PAC-12 institution.

On the way toward self-sufficiency, we are seeking a 25% pay increase by AY 2017-2018 in addition to regional cost of living adjustment.

We demand a binding written commitment from the Board of Regents by November 1, 2016.

Full tuition waiver

CU-Boulder's graduate employees often receive less than full funding, even while performing work comparable to their fully-funded peers.

We are seeking full tuition remission for all graduate employees by AY 2017-2018.

We demand a binding written commitment from the Board of Regents by November 1, 2016.

Transparency & inclusion

CU Boulder has delayed and censored the Graduate Student Social Climate Survey, obstructing efforts to better include underrepresented populations.

To enable graduate directed improvement of the university's social climate, we are seeking speedy and uncensored survey analysis by a trusted third party.

We demand contract with a mutually agreed third party signed by October 1, 2016.
