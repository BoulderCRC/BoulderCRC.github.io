CU-Boulder CRC

Committee on Rights and Compensation

advancing CU-Boulder's core missions by
improving graduate employee work conditions

  • Tell Me More

CRC is making CU-Boulder a better university
by seeking equitable work conditions for graduate employees.

Graduate employees (including TAs, RAs, and GPTIs) perform a great portion of academic labor that is
fundamental to CU-Boulder's statutory mission to be a world-class research and education university.

We envision a fair, inclusive, and democratic work environment for these workers.

Goals for Fall 2016

Equitable pay

CU-Boulder pays its graduate employees approximately 65% of regional self-sufficiency wage, which is less than any other PAC-12 institution.

On the way toward full self-sufficiency, we are seeking a 25% pay increase by AY 2017-2018 in addition to regional cost of living adjustment.

We require a written commitment signed by the Provost and Chancellor by November 1, 2016.

Full tuition waiver

CU-Boulder's graduate employees often receive less than full funding, even when they perform work comparable to their fully-funded peers.

To minimize the financial burden of lowball appointment percentages, we are seeking full tuition remission for all graduate employees by AY 2017-2018.

We require a written commitment signed by the Provost and Chancellor by November 1, 2016.

Transparent inclusion

CU Boulder has delayed and censored the Graduate Student Social Climate Survey, obstructing efforts to include underrepresented populations better.

To enable grassroots improvement of the university's social climate, we are seeking speedy and uncensored survey analysis by a trusted third party.

We expect an agreeable contract with a mutually agreeable third party signed by November 1, 2016.
